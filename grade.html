Cinnamon Grades (Cinnamomum zeylanicum)

Typology and Grading Standard Specifications of True Cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum)

Alba

Representing the apex of commercial cinnamon classification, the Alba grade is strictly defined by a maximum cross-sectional diameter of 6.0 mm. It features an exceptionally high degree of friability and a delicate, uniform texture. Organoleptically, it is highly prized for its profound aromatic profile and pronounced sweet flavor vector, driven by an optimal volatile oil composition.

Continental (C5-Special, C5, C4)

A globally standardized, premium export commodity class. Quills within the C-series exhibit superior structural integrity, mechanical firmness, and a characteristic golden-yellow chromaticity. Delineated primarily by quill diameter (ranging from approximately 8.0 mm to 16.0 mm) and the minimal presence of cortical blemishes, this grade offers an optimal balance of structural resilience and aromatic potency.

Mexican (M5, M4)

A mid-tier commercial classification characterized by increased surface roughness, minor bark variations, and marginally darker pigmentation compared to the C-series. The M-series is a highly strategic export grade tailored for high-volume consumption. It predominantly supplies Latin American commodity markets while maintaining significant utilization within the Australian industrial food processing sector due to its highly favorable cost-to-flavor extraction ratio.

Hamburg (H1, H2, H3)

This classification encompasses the thickest and most robust cortical layers (typically exceeding 18.0 mm in diameter). Due to its high structural density, lower friability, and morphological coarseness, the H-series is rarely retailed in its whole-quill form. Instead, it serves as the primary botanical substrate for industrial comminution (pulverization into ground cinnamon) and the steam distillation or solvent extraction of cinnamaldehyde-rich essential oils.

Quillings/Chips

Classified as the lowest commercial grade, quillings and chips are by-products of the primary quill fabrication process. They consist of fragmented bark pieces, untrimmed branch segments, and peeling residues that do not meet the dimensional or aesthetic criteria of higher grades. Despite their reduced structural integrity and organoleptic intensity, these fractions retain significant biochemical value and are predominantly utilized for large-scale essential oil extraction, oleoresin production, and bulk powder manufacturing.
